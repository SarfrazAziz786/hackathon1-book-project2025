# Module 2: The Digital Twin (Gazebo & Unity)

This module covers building digital twins for humanoid robots using Gazebo and Unity, with emphasis on physics simulation, sensor modeling, and human-robot interaction.

Target audience: AI and robotics students with basic ROS 2 and simulation knowledge.

In this module, you will learn:
- The concept of a digital twin in robotics
- Physics simulation (gravity, collisions) in Gazebo
- The role of Unity for high-fidelity rendering and interaction
- How LiDAR, depth cameras, and IMUs are simulated

## Chapter Overview

1. [Digital Twin Concept](./chapter-1-digital-twin-concept.md) - Understanding the digital twin paradigm in robotics
2. [Gazebo Physics Simulation](./chapter-2-gazebo-physics.md) - Physics simulation for robot environments
3. [Unity for Rendering](./chapter-3-unity-rendering.md) - High-fidelity rendering and interaction in Unity

## Module Structure

This module is structured to provide a comprehensive understanding of digital twin technology for robotics. We begin with the conceptual framework of digital twins, proceed to practical implementation using Gazebo for physics simulation, and conclude with Unity for high-fidelity rendering.

The concept of a digital twin is fundamental to modern robotics development, enabling safer, more cost-effective development and testing of robotic systems. This approach has become increasingly important as robotic systems become more complex and expensive to test in the physical world.

## Success Criteria

After completing this module, you should be able to:
- Define what constitutes a digital twin in robotics
- Recognize the differences between digital twins and traditional simulations
- Understand applications of digital twins in robotics
- Configure physics parameters in Gazebo for realistic simulation
- Understand how collision detection and response work
- Recognize scenarios where Unity is preferred over Gazebo for visualization
- Understand how to visualize human-robot interaction in Unity

## Chapter Summaries

### Chapter 1: Digital Twin Concept
This chapter introduces the concept of a digital twin in robotics, explaining how it differs from other simulation approaches and its role in robot development and testing. You'll learn about the benefits and applications of digital twins and how they integrate with physical robot systems.

### Chapter 2: Gazebo Physics Simulation
This chapter covers physics simulation in Gazebo, including gravity, collisions, friction, and other physical properties that affect robot behavior in simulation. You'll learn how to configure physics parameters to achieve realistic robot simulation.

### Chapter 3: Unity for Rendering
This chapter explores Unity's role in high-fidelity rendering and interaction, highlighting its advantages over other rendering solutions for digital twins. You'll learn about visualization techniques and how to create immersive environments for human-robot interaction studies.
